ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT STUDY REPORT
FOR THE PROPOSED INCREASE OF STEVIA COUNTIES IN NANDI, LAIKIPIA, BUNGOMA, UASIN NGISHU, NAROK AND MERU
INVITATION OF PUBLIC COMMENTS
PURSUANT to regulation 21 of Environmental (Impact Assessment and Audit) Regulations, the National Environment Management Authority, (NEMA) has received an environmental impact assessment study report for the implementation of the proposed increase of stevia counties.
The proposed development entails establishment of stevia farming in the counties of Nandi, Laikipia, Bungoma, Uasin-Gishu, Narok and Meru.
The following are the anticipated impacts and proposed mitigation measures:

Possible Impact


Mitigation measures


Diversification on Income Generating Activities


Periodic on-site training to maintain the tempo for sustainability.
Ensure all inputs are applied for optimum production.


Mismanagement of the enterprise due to increased workload


Analysis of Labour profiles for all possible enterprise mixes, including examining the daily gender calendar for the target community.
Youth to be included as they form the bulk of the rural labour force.
Suitability of leaf threshing for the older folk.


Technical knowledge
 


Proponent to engage own specialized extension personnel and continuous induction of Ministry of Agriculture extension staff on the Stevia husbandry.
Training of extension officers in Stevia production will be necessary.


Food security
 


Insist on appropriate enterprise mix, including livestock, food crops and cash crops.


Crop–Water demand


Contour planting with the correct spacing to increase water infiltration.
Swallow irrigation in case of dry conditions.
Need to determine the crop-water demand at varying conditions.


Loss of soil fertility


Contour tillage operations on areas that have slopes and incorporating leaves into the soil. Use of compost manure to be encouraged.
Planting to be in rolling terrain for adequate erosion control.


Waste disposal system


Improve the disposal system through composting.


Monocrop


Allow a corridor of sesbania sesban  along the pathway and at the boundarie.
This shall provide wind break and other affiliated eco-functions of trees.


Infrastructure development


Improve the rural roads network.
Proponent and local leaders team up in improving the road network.
